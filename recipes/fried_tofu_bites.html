Fried Tofu Bites

I created this recipe for my boyfriend who loves fried tofu.
I wanted them bite sized so they could be put into any dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 block of extra firm tofu
  • 1 cup of panko bread crumbs
  • 1 cup of flour
  • 1 can of chickpea liquid or 2 eggs whisked
  • Oil for frying
  • Optional: seasoning for breading

Steps

  1. Drain tofu, but don't press or fully dry it (having it still slightly wet will help the flour stick to the pieces better)
  2. Cut the tofu into bite size cubes
  3. Get out 3 bowls
    • 1 bowl with flour
    • 1 bowl with chickpea liquid (or egg)
    • 1 bowl with bread crumbs (add seasoning if you want extra flavor)

    *You may need to add more to each bowl if you start running low

  4. Add oil to a pan (about Â¼ of an inch deep) and turn heat to medium/medium high
  5. Take one or two pieces of the tofu cubes and roll them around in the flour so all sides are coated and they feel dry
  6. Dunk those pieces into the chickpea liquid or eggs
  7. Drop those pieces into the bread crumbs and coat all sides

    8. *You can bread all the pieces first and set them on a plate or bread them as you go

  8. Once the oil is hot and you have your pieces breaded, start placing them in the pan, leaving some room to flip them over (I use a fork for this)
  9. Flip each tofu bite over when the bottom becomes golden brown (about 2-3 minutes each side but also depending on your heat)

    10. *If the pieces are browning too quickly or getting too dark, turn the heat down

  10. Remove each piece from the pan and place on a plate lined with a paper towel
  11. EAT THOSE SWEET CRISPY BABIES!
